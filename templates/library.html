The Core Principles: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Reduce

The most important 'R'. It means **minimizing the amount of waste** you create in the first place. Choose products with less packaging, buy bulk, and avoid single-use plastics.

üîÑ

Reuse

Before throwing something away, find a way to **use it again**. This includes donating old clothes, using jars as containers, and repurposing items for crafts or new functions.

‚ôªÔ∏è

Recycle

Turning waste materials into **new products** to prevent the consumption of fresh raw materials. Ensure you know your local rules for separating plastics, paper, glass, and metals.

Aluminium Power

Recycling aluminum saves **95% of the energy** required to produce the same amount of metal from raw ore. A can is back on the shelf in as little as 60 days!

üå≥

Save Trees, Save Water

Recycling one ton of paper saves about **17 mature trees** and 7,000 gallons of water. Always shred sensitive documents before recycling to maintain privacy.

üåä

Plastic Longevity

Most plastics take between **400 to 1,000 years** to decompose in landfills. Correct sorting is crucial, as different plastic types (PET, HDPE, etc.) require different processing.
